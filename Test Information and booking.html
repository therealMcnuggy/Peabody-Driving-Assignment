Test Information

Theory test

The driving theory test is the first part of the governments requirements to see whether you are able to drive. The test is used to see whether you have the driving knowledge and hazard perception skills required to drive a car. It is made up of three different sections comprising of multiple choice questions, video case studys and hazard perception.
In order to pass the multiple choice question section you will get 50 questions to answer with 43 of them needing to be correct for you to pass.
The video case studys will have 3 multiple choice questions that you will need to answer.
To pass the hazard perception section you will need to watch 14 video clips and identify the hazard seen in them, at least 44 of the 75 total hazards need to be identified to pass.
This insurance covers you for anything that migh happen when you are completing the practical driving tests.



Practical test

The Practical driving test is the second part of the governments requirements to see whether you are able to drive. The test is comprised of many parts including an eyesight test, a "show me tell me" test where you will need to show that you have a basic understanding about the engine, the maneuver/emergency stop test and finaly the actual driving for around 40 mins including the 10 mins of solo driving.
